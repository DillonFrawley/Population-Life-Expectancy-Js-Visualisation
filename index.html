Average Life Expectancy & Gender Difference Per Continent & Country in 2019



Average Life Expectancy



Africa has the largest range of average life expectancy for a continent with some countries such as Algeria, Morocco and Libya having average life expectancies higher than the mean average life expectancy for the entire world(72.5 years) but Africa also contains countries well below the average life expectancy such as the Central African Republic having an Average life expectancy of only 53.3 years which makes it the country with the lowest average life expectancy in the world.

The continent with the highest average life expectancy is Europe being 79.9 years.

Interestingly Europe also contains the country having the highest average life expectancy in the world being Europe with an average life expectancy of 85.4 years

Oceania is an interesting continent to analyse when looking at average life expectancy, Australia and New Zealand being very wealthy and developed countries have above the mean average life expectancy for the entire world(72.5 years) and for the continent of Oceania(70.1 years) withAustralia having an average life expectancy of 83.4 years and New Zealand having and average life expectancy of 82.3 years.



Out of the top ten countries in the world by average life expectancy we can see in figure 2 that 7 out of 10 are from Europe. As previously mentioned in figure 1 the country with the highest average life expectancy in the world is Monaco with 85.9 years.

Australia is the only country in the top ten from Oceania with an average life expectancy of 83.4 years and is the clear outlier in figure 2.

As previously mentioned in figure 1 Africa contains the country with the lowest average life expectancy in the world being the Central African Republic (53.3 years). Intrestingly, for the 10 worst ranked countries by average life expectancy all 10 countries are from Africa, which when looking at the average life expectancy for each continent one would not be surprised that Africa has the lowest average life expectancy being only 63.9 years which is 8.6 years below the average life expectancy for the entire world.



Gender Difference




Interestingly Europe being the continent with the highest average life expectancy also has the most diverse scale of gender difference. Iceland has the lowest gender difference in Europe with a gender difference of only 3 years whereas as we can see in figure 4 Lithuania the country with the largest gender difference in the world being 11.1 years.

Russia has quite a large gender difference and contains the second largest gender difference in the world with 10.6 years.

The mean gender difference for the entire world is 4.9 years, meaning females live on average 4.9 years longer than males

As we can see in figure 5 there is no country in the world that has a higher male average life expectancy than female average life expectancy. The country with the least gender difference is from the continent Asia and the country is Bhutan with the male average life expectancy being 71.4 years and the female average life expectancy being 72.2 years.

In South America the country of Venezuela has the highest gender difference with the male average life expectancy being 68.3 years and the female average life expectancy being 76 years.
